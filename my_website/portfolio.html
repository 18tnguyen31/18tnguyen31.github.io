Hey Widdly Guitars

Game Reflection

The purpose of my game is to score as many points as possible, and points are earned by
accurately pressing on the corresponding arrows to when the guitars hit the "scoring row."
To make my game, I started off with the technique to score points. It began with how the guitars
would move upward, and it progressed to the points which would be added. When start is received, the
guitars will return to their original position then glide upward in the time determined by the difficulty.
To score, the player would have to have the correct arrow key pressed when the moving guitars are touching
the stationary ones. If the key is correctly pressed, the score is changed by +10. When it's pressed and not
touching, it would subtract 5 from the score. The most difficult thing about my game was moving the guitars.
I solved this issue by "taking inspiration" from another game. I had an opportunity to make a dank ending
screen so I used presidents :/


Happy Timmy

Quick Summary

Happy Timmy is about a happy boy who gets a good SAT score. He has the choice to go to college or take
a gap year but no matter what happens, there's a sad ending.

Story Reflection

To produce this story, the basic programming concepts we needed were if else statements, making
functions, comparisons, and calling forth other functions. At first, we made a very long and complicated
if-else statement with multiple if-else statements inside, however it just got too confusing. With some
help from Ms. Terry, we found out that you can just make a bunch of functions and call
them based off of an input. I'm kind of proud of this story, because it seemed to get
a great reaction based on the turn of events. The title is pretty ironic and the whole idea of things
ending badly kind of reminds me of Game of Thrones, which I happen to love.
A story which I particularly liked was "Watch Out Watch Out Watch Out!!" because your demise mainly
comes from an RKO. If I were to redo this story, I would add more decision points and make more
of a lengthy story. I interpretted this assignment as more of a short story and some took it as writing
a book. I'd like to have made a better medium, where the story is not super short or super long.
